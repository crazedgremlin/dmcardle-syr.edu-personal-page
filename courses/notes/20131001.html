Mathematical Proofs: The Big Picture

Proof by Contraposition

A conditional $q \rightarrow r$ is logically equivalent to its contrapositive $\neg r \rightarrow \neg q$.

To show $$H_1 \wedge H_2 \wedge \ldots \wedge H_k \rightarrow C$$ you must show that the contrapositive is true: $$\neg C \rightarrow \neg (H_1 \wedge H_2 \wedge \ldots \wedge H_k )$$

Proof by Contradiction

$$ \begin{align} &p \rightarrow \text{FALSE}\\ &\therefore \neg p \end{align} $$ To show $H_1 \wedge H_2 \wedge \ldots \wedge H_k \rightarrow C $ show that $$(H_1 \wedge H_2 \wedge \ldots \wedge H_k \wedge \neg C) \rightarrow \text{FALSE} $$
