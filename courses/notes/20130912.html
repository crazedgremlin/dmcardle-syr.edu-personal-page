Example HW Problem:

$ \exists x \in U, P(x) \\ \exists x \in U, Q(x) \\ \therefore \exists x \in U, (P(x) \wedge Q(x)) $

To show it's invalid, we want to show that

$ \eqalign{ \exists x \in U, P(x) & \equiv \color{green}{T} \\ \exists x \in U, Q(x) & \equiv \color{green}{T}\\ \therefore \exists x \in U, (P(x) \wedge Q(x)) & \equiv \color{red}{F} } $

Remember that $$ \eqalign{ \exists x \in U, (P(x) \wedge Q(x)) & \equiv \color{red}{F} \\ \neg ( \exists x \in U, (P(x) \wedge Q(x))) & \equiv \color{green}{T} \\ } \\ \eqalign{ \forall x & \in U, \neg(P(x) \wedge Q(x)) \\ \forall x & \in U, \neg P(x) \vee \neg Q(x) } $$ In summary, if you can show that either $P$ is false or $Q$ is false for each $x \in U$, you have shown that the argument form is invalid.

New Rules

Universal Instantiation

$ \forall x \in U, p(x) \\ a \in U \\ \therefore p(a) $

Example

$ \forall n \in W, (P(n) \rightarrow Q(n)) \\ 5 \in W \\ P(5) \\ \therefore Q(5) $

Universal Generalization

  • Take an arbitrary element $a \in U$
  • Establish that $p(a)$ holds
The conclusion $\forall x \in U, p(x)$ holds.

Example

Show that the following is a valid argument:

$ \forall x \in U, (P(x) \rightarrow Q(x)) \\ \forall x \in U, P(x) \\ \therefore \forall x \in U, Q(x) $

Existential Generalization

$ p(a) \\ a \in U \\ \therefore \exists a \in U, p(a) $

Existential Instantiation

$ \exists x \in U, P(x)\\ \therefore P(a) $

Note that $a$ must be a fresh variable, not previously used in the proof.
